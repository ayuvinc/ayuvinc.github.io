The Problem

Healthcare treats visits.
Not patients.

Every time a patient changes doctor, clinic, or city — their medical history stays behind. Every new doctor starts from zero. India's healthcare system has no memory.

600K+

Independent clinics in India, each owning their own data silo — disconnected from each other and from the patient.

3B+

Prescriptions written every year in India — nearly all on paper, with no audit trail, no continuity, no intelligence.

Zero

Longitudinal patient records across India's independent clinic network. Every new appointment starts from scratch.

"For 17 years, I was my own medical record system."

— Aditya Kaushal, Founder & CEO

Who We Are

Three founders.
Three failures of the same system.

We didn't find this problem in a market report. We lived it. A CFE, an enterprise architect, and a clinician — each with a personal reason to build this.

Aditya Kaushal
Founder & CEO

CFE · 13+ years forensic investigations at Deloitte, PwC, Optum. Patient Zero of Dhara.

"At 17, my kidneys failed. My father gave me his. That kidney failed. I was alone in a Tunisian ICU in 2022 — my records were in India, on paper. My mother gave me her kidney in 2024 after an 11-month wait. For 17 years, I was my own medical record system. I built this because I lived every failure it fixes."
Raghu Raja Sharma
Co-Founder & CTO

Enterprise architect · 14+ years across 7 countries · JP Morgan, Cigna, HSBC · TOGAF certified.

"On the day my son was born, my mother had a stroke. I had built enterprise systems that moved billions across 7 countries. I had never felt more helpless than navigating a hospital with no map, no record, no system."
Dr. Komal Sharma
Co-Founder & Chief Clinical Officer

MD Dermatology · Medanta Fellowship · Consultant, Healing Hospital Chandigarh.

"Every day in clinic, my patients bring years of history in plastic bags — photographs of old prescriptions, half-remembered diagnoses. They have seen seven doctors and none of them knew what the others said. The Doctor Approval Gate is my clinical principle: AI is a tool, not a decision-maker."

"We are not building an EMR. We are building the layer that connects India's 600,000 clinics around the patient."
