Why This Exists

The Problem

India's independent clinic network is the largest undigitised healthcare delivery system in the world. Six hundred thousand independent doctors handle the majority of the country's outpatient care. They run on prescription pads.

  • Infrastructure gap at scale. Fewer than 2% of India's independent clinics operate functioning EMR. 600 million outpatient consultations per year. Zero longitudinal record.
  • Patient data is imprisoned, not fragmented. Every existing platform stores records under institutional ownership — the doctor owns the record, not the patient. Every new doctor starts from scratch.
  • Documentation burden is breaking clinic workflows. The average independent doctor spends 4–6 minutes per consultation on paperwork. For a 50-patient OPD day, that is 4+ hours of documentation.
  • The transaction layer is unmonetised and non-compliant. Pharmacy and pathology transaction value across India's independent clinic network exceeds ₹800 Crore annually. Every platform that tried to monetise this violated 2002 MCI Ethics Regulations, Section 6.4 — currently operative; NMC 2023 regulations in abeyance. The compliance gap is structural.

The founder's experience is not an anecdote — it is the product specification. Aditya Kaushal has had two kidney transplants. For twenty years, every clinic visit began with reciting every medication, every surgery, every allergic reaction, every prior test — from memory. Every single time. Dhara exists so every patient has it, not just the patients who can remember.

Product

The Solution

Dhara is a three-module clinical operating system for India's independent clinics. It is an open platform — clinics keep their existing tools. Dhara layers on top, starting with the highest-friction problem the doctor has today.

01 · EMR

Dhara

₹199/month ($2.21) · 3 months free

Patient-owned longitudinal health records — UUID-native, ABHA-bridgeable on consent, FHIR R4 native with ICD-10 coding. Every consultation, prescription, lab result, and adherence event flows into a record the patient owns and carries across every provider.

Stickiness: three months of active use makes switching cost effectively prohibitive. The free trial is the retention mechanism.

02 · AI Scribe

Vaani

₹46.875/session GP ($0.52) · ₹140.625/session Psych ($1.56)

AI clinical scribe built on Sarvam AI — natively trained on Indian languages. Real-time multilingual transcription and SOAP note structuring during the consultation. Every AI output is a suggestion — nothing confirms without explicit doctor approval.

Psychiatry first: 45-minute sessions, 70% attachment rate, 3× per-session revenue vs GP. Primary revenue driver.

03 · Routing

Transaction Routing

₹31/fill pharmacy ($0.34) · ₹71/referral pathology ($0.79)

Pharmacy and pathology transaction routing — turns each prescription into a coordinated, tracked, consent-gated event. Patient pays on the Dhara platform; AyuVinc settles with the pharmacy and lab. UPI collect-first model: AyuVinc always holds the float.

NMC compliance as architecture: 100% of routing commission flows to AyuVinc. Zero to the clinician. No competitor can retrofit this without a full rebuild.

Open platform architecture — clinics keep their existing tools. Multiple pharmacy chains and lab partners compete for orders on auditable KPIs: fulfillment rate, proximity, speed, price. No partner can pay for preferential placement.

Technology & Compliance

Architecture Built for the Regulation

The architecture is built to the standard the regulation will eventually require. The certification follows the architecture — not the other way around.

ABDM / FHIR Architecture

The Dhara codebase is FHIR R4 native with ICD-10 coding. Patient identity is UUID-native and ABHA-bridgeable on patient consent. ABDM M1 certification is a Y1 Q3–Q4 milestone — it is not required for commercial launch. AyuVinc's Year 1 target market (independent private clinics) is not under ABDM mandate in 2026. Architecture readiness: 8/10. ABDM certification readiness: 3/10 — deliberately. The integration layer starts post-commercial launch.

"ABDM-aligned by architecture. M1 certification in progress — target Y1 Q3. Patient data is FHIR R4 native from day one. The certificate follows the architecture."

Sarvam AI — Clinical Scribe Backbone

Vaani is built on Sarvam AI — the only Indian-language foundation model natively trained on Indian clinical speech. Current COGS: ₹80/hr (conservative model buffer). Sarvam's actual published rate: ₹65/hr. The ₹15/hr gap flows directly to Vaani gross margin on any volume pricing deal post-PGI LOI.

Dual-track strategy post-PGI LOI: Sarvam AI (volume pricing, integration partnership) and Google (cloud infrastructure support, replaces AWS line) run in parallel. Any deal at or below ₹80/hr is pure margin upside — the model does not count on it.

Offline / Queued Mode

Clinic connectivity failure is not a product failure. Sarvam Edge (294MB, <300ms) captures audio locally when connectivity drops. Raw transcript queues and structures when connectivity returns — note available within minutes of reconnection. Prescriptions queue locally with paper fallback. Patient record summaries cached on-device as read-only (25MB encrypted). This constraint is solved at the architecture level before commercial launch.

What Seed Investors Are Underwriting

The seed round is not funding product-market fit discovery. It is funding the construction of an asset that Series A investors will pay a premium to access. That asset is the data layer.

  • 5,000+ EBITDA-positive clinics — generating ₹10,138/month each at maturity. The platform is cash positive before the first Series A rupee lands.

Series A investors are not paying for AyuVinc's product. They are paying for the network — 5,000 clinic relationships, 5M+ encounters, and a data moat that took 36 months and ₹9 Crore to build.
