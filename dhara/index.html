Overview
Executive Summary
The raise, the model, and what it buys.

AyuVinc is building Dhara — India's AI-powered Clinical Operating System for independent doctors. We are raising ₹9 Crore ($1.0M) at ₹60 Crore ($6.7M) pre-money to prove the model in 300 clinics, reach EBITDA positive at Month 22, and establish the data layer that makes us the default infrastructure for India's 600,000 independent doctors.

India's independent clinic market is 600,000 strong and entirely undigitised. Fewer than 2% of independent clinics run functioning EMR. The gap is not technology — it is architecture: every prior platform imprisoned patient data under institutional ownership, built compliance-violating commission structures, and charged prices that excluded Tier 2–3 clinics. Dhara is built differently from the ground up.

02
Why This Exists
The Problem
600,000 doctors. 600 million consultations. Zero longitudinal record.

India's independent clinic network is the largest undigitised healthcare delivery system in the world. Six hundred thousand independent doctors handle the majority of the country's outpatient care. They run on prescription pads.

  • Infrastructure gap at scale. Fewer than 2% of India's independent clinics operate functioning EMR. 600 million outpatient consultations per year. Zero longitudinal record.
  • Patient data is imprisoned, not fragmented. Every existing platform stores records under institutional ownership — the doctor owns the record, not the patient. Every new doctor starts from scratch.
  • Documentation burden is breaking clinic workflows. The average independent doctor spends 4–6 minutes per consultation on paperwork. For a 50-patient OPD day, that is 4+ hours of documentation.
  • The transaction layer is unmonetised and non-compliant. Pharmacy and pathology transaction value across India's independent clinic network exceeds ₹800 Crore annually. Every platform that tried to monetise this violated 2002 MCI Ethics Regulations, Section 6.4 — currently operative; NMC 2023 regulations in abeyance. The compliance gap is structural.

The founder's experience is not an anecdote — it is the product specification. Aditya Kaushal has had two kidney transplants. For twenty years, every clinic visit began with reciting every medication, every surgery, every allergic reaction, every prior test — from memory. Every single time. Dhara exists so every patient has it, not just the patients who can remember.

03
Product
The Solution
Three modules. One open platform. Layered onto existing clinic workflows.

Dhara is a three-module clinical operating system for India's independent clinics. It is an open platform — clinics keep their existing tools. Dhara layers on top, starting with the highest-friction problem the doctor has today.

01 · EMR

Dhara

₹199/month ($2.21) · 3 months free

Patient-owned longitudinal health records — UUID-native, ABHA-bridgeable on consent, FHIR R4 native with ICD-10 coding. Every consultation, prescription, lab result, and adherence event flows into a record the patient owns and carries across every provider.

Switching cost after 90 days of structured records is effectively prohibitive.
02 · AI Scribe

Vaani

₹46.875/session GP ($0.52) · ₹140.625/session Psych ($1.56)

AI clinical scribe built on Sarvam AI — natively trained on Indian languages. Real-time multilingual transcription and SOAP note structuring. Every AI output is a suggestion — nothing confirms without explicit doctor approval.

Psychiatry: 70% attach rate, 45-min sessions. Primary revenue driver.
03 · Routing

Transaction Routing

₹31/fill pharmacy ($0.34) · ₹71/referral pathology ($0.79)

Pharmacy and pathology transaction routing — each prescription becomes a coordinated, tracked, consent-gated event. Patient pays on the Dhara platform; AyuVinc settles with pharmacy and lab. UPI collect-first: AyuVinc always holds the float.

100% commission to AyuVinc. Zero to clinician. Architecture-level MCI compliance.
Open platform architecture: clinics keep their existing tools. Multiple pharmacy chains and lab partners compete on auditable KPIs — fulfillment rate, proximity, speed, price. No partner can pay for preferential placement.

07
Technology & Compliance
Architecture Built for the Regulation
FHIR R4 native · Sarvam Edge offline · DPDP-ready from day one.

ABDM / FHIR Architecture

The Dhara codebase is FHIR R4 native with ICD-10 coding. Patient identity is UUID-native and ABHA-bridgeable on patient consent. ABDM M1 certification is a Y1 Q3–Q4 milestone — it is not required for commercial launch. Architecture readiness: 8/10. ABDM certification readiness: 3/10 — deliberately. The integration layer starts post-commercial launch.

Sarvam AI — Clinical Scribe Backbone

Vaani is built on Sarvam AI — the only Indian-language foundation model natively trained on Indian clinical speech. Modelled COGS: ₹80/hr. Actual published rate: ₹65/hr. The ₹15/hr gap is pure margin upside on any volume pricing deal post-PGI LOI. Dual-track post-PGI LOI: Sarvam (volume pricing) and Google (cloud infrastructure, replaces AWS line) run in parallel.

Offline / Queued Mode

Sarvam Edge (294MB, <300ms) captures audio locally when connectivity drops. Transcript queues and structures on reconnection. Prescriptions queue with paper fallback. Patient summaries cached on-device (25MB encrypted). Connectivity failure is not a product failure.

Series A
What Seed Investors Are Underwriting
The data layer. Not product-market fit — that is already proven at M22.

The seed round is not funding product-market fit discovery. It is funding the construction of an asset that Series A investors will pay a premium to access. That asset is the data layer.

What 5M+ structured encounters unlock at Series A

  • 5,000+ EBITDA-positive clinics — generating ₹10,138/month each at maturity. Platform cash positive before the first Series A rupee lands.
  • 5M+ structured patient encounters — consented, FHIR R4 native, ICD-10 coded. Independent clinic data — the segment every national health dataset misses.
  • AI lab under training — AI Researcher hired at M12, training on the largest consented independent clinic dataset in India. Frontier lab partnership conversations become possible because the data quality and consent architecture are unprecedented.
  • Specialty module roadmap — dialysis, oncology, ICU, emergency. Each a new revenue stream layered onto existing clinic relationships. The infrastructure already exists; the modules are additive.
  • Sarvam AI and Google Cloud — PGI LOI transforms cold vendor calls into partnership negotiations. Volume pricing drops COGS below ₹65/hr. Google Cloud infrastructure support removes the AWS line.

Series A investors are not paying for AyuVinc's product. They are paying for the network — 5,000 clinic relationships, 5M+ encounters, and a data moat that took 36 months and ₹9 Crore ($1.0M) to build. No competitor can replicate that dataset in the time it would take to raise and deploy a comparable round.
